Biography

Early Life

I was born in Portland, Oregon in the early 80's. From an early age I got into computers and music and all through childhood those were my hobbies, and I also did pretty well in school. In my senior year of high school I was in student leadership, had a star roll on the mock trial team, and was enrolled at Reed College as pre-freshman student. Unfortunately, as it later turned out, I was also in a professional jazz band and before one year of college was over I quit school to be a musician full time.

Music Career

For some 15 years or so I lived as a professional musician playing gigs, busking, and teaching lessons. During this time I learned a lot about life, especially what not to do! I also learned about running a business and dealing with clients. Eventually I gave up the dream and decided to switch my hobby (computers) with my profession (music).

Developing Developer...

In the winter of 2019 I enrolled in a C++ class at a community college and after a life of tinkering I started to become a serious programmer. After the challenging initial learning curve I finished the class materials before the term was over and then finished the text book on my own. I didn't enroll in any further classes and continued studying on my own. Since then I've spent several hours a day learning and developing.

Skills and Talents

Like most new developers, pretty much everything I know is in the realm of web development. I've messed around with various frontend frameworks and especially like Vue, though I don't mind React or Angular. My 'expertise', such as it is, is in React and Vue though I can rise to the occasion of any frontend task I might be asked to do and I'm not shy of backend stuff either. Lately I've been getting nitty-gritty with raw CSS. I can learn whatever stack my team uses in requisite time. Most recently I figured out how to make a full-featured progressive web app with React + Workbox and published a tutorial about it.

I have soft skills. Which means I can follow instructions and get along with a team. Hopefully the benefits of life experience will make this mid-life career change of mine go a little smoother.

After working hard and consistently the past few years to make myself into a developer I'm ready to start my career. I'd like to get a job somewhere as a junior developer but I am open to doing an internship or help out by doing some freelance work. I'd even be willing to do a free website/app or two, so please contact me if I can be useful to you or someone you know.
